Introduction

We use relative clauses to give more information about people, things, or places:

  • who: for people - The boy who is wearing a red hat is my friend.
  • which: for things - The book which is on the table is mine.
  • where: for places - The park where we play is near my house.

Relative clauses help us join sentences together and make our English more interesting!
